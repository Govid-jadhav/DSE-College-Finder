Frequently Asked Questions

What is Direct Second Year (DSE) Admission? ▼

It is a lateral entry admission system in Maharashtra allowing engineering diploma holders and B.Sc. graduates to join directly in the 3rd semester (2nd year) of undergraduate Bachelor of Engineering (B.E./B.Tech) courses.

What does Dream, Match, and Safe categories mean? ▼

Dream options represent colleges with cutoffs 0-2% higher than your score (highly competitive). Match options are realistic matches within 0-5% below your score. Safe options have cutoffs more than 5% below your score (high seat probability).

Why does the predictor say "Locked"? ▼

To unlock recommendations, you must sign in to save your shortlist and submit a short rating/feedback review on the reviews page. This helps maintain community guidelines and gathers feedback to refine prediction algorithms.

What is the Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) and am I eligible? ▼

TFWS provides 100% waiver of tuition fees for students. To be eligible, your annual family income must be strictly less than ₹8 Lakhs from all sources, and you must apply specifically for TFWS seats during the CAP registration.

How is the aggregate diploma percentage calculated for DSE merit numbers? ▼

The aggregate percentage is calculated based on the marks obtained in the final year (usually the 5th and 6th semesters) of your diploma program, as defined by MSBTE or your respective autonomous board.

Can I change my engineering branch during Direct Second Year admission? ▼

Yes, students can change their engineering branch (e.g. from Mechanical Diploma to Computer Engineering degree) subject to branch-eligibility rules specified by the DTE and seat availability.

Is a Caste Validity Certificate mandatory at the time of option entry? ▼

Yes, the Caste Validity Certificate is mandatory. While DTE may allow uploads of confirmation receipts during registration, the final original certificate must be presented at the Admission Reporting Center (ARC) to secure admission under reservation.

What is the difference between Freeze, Float, and Slide options? ▼

"Freeze" accepts the allotted seat and locks it (no further CAP round participation). "Float" accepts the seat but keeps options open for better choices in subsequent rounds. "Slide" accepts the seat but only looks for better branches within the same college.

What are Minority Seats and who can claim them? ▼

Minority seats are reserved in specific colleges for linguistic (e.g., Hindi, Sindhi, Gujarati) or religious (e.g., Muslim, Christian, Sikh) minorities. Candidates belonging to these groups must select their minority category during DTE registration to qualify.
